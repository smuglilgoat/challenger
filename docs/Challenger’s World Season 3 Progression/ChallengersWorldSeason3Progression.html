Boss Ranking Tier List

I am going to put all of the required Challenger bosses in certain tiers, which will mainly identify how much damage you need to be able to clear them. Bosses in the later tiers unfortunately will require a certain extent of knowledge on the boss so I strongly recommend looking up guides for bosses you are unsure about defeating.

Tier 1: Free Bosses

The earliest stage of the game. These bosses are extremely easy and can be completely annihilated simply with just item burning and the free boss accessories you are given. Kill all of these bosses and reach Level 260 and you’re already Bronze Tier. Lotus & Damien at the end only includes their Normal versions.

Tier 2: A Small Challenge Begins

Normal Slime, Easy Lucid, & Easy Will. They are a significant step up compared to the tier 1 bosses, but with enough knowledge on these bosses I believe they are huge push overs after you have unlocked your 6th job advancement and upgraded your item burning as much as possible.

Tier 3: Still not so bad

Normal Lucid, Normal Will, Normal Gloom, & Normal Darknell. These are the final bosses before you start getting into a little bit of harder territory. While posing a bit more of a challenge compared to the previous tier, I believe they are still push overs with the same equipment, but obviously not as much. Just make sure you have enough Arcane Force for everything, as their requirements in this tier go up quite a bit.

Tier 4: A Real Challenge

Hard Lotus, Hard Damien, & Normal Verus Hilla. This is your first step into Hard Boss Territory, and these are the easy ones. I would not recommend until you are a bit of the way until 6th Job and around Level 266+ with how fast leveling is. These bosses can still be done with the free boss accessories and Item Burning but it would pose a real challenge and requires genuine knowledge on all of these bosses and good endurance.

By this time you should have the free Ring of Restraint or Continuous Ring from the Challenger UI so it wouldn’t be so bad, but it’s best to be prepared.

Tier 5: The Gauntlet

Chaos Slime, Hard Lucid, Hard Will, Chaos Gloom, Hard Verus Hilla, Hard Darknell, & Normal Kai. Soloing these bosses is one of the biggest milestones you can have as a first time or returning player. For this stage, I recommend you are at least Level 270+, and you may even have to borrow some Rental Gear if you want to do this early on. With how powerful the Rental Gear is, every boss in this tier is absolutely doable with it equipped.

Gear alone won’t carry you fully, though. You’ll need to have enough knowledge on both the bosses and how your class plays to be able to succeed.

Tier 6: Early Grandis

Normal Seren, Easy Kalos, & Easy Adversary. These are the entry bosses to the Grandis region, which is the current endgame region of MapleStory. You are introduced to bosses with mechanics far more complex than any of the bosses you have faced until now (except Normal Kai since it’s a new boss).

You’ll need a significant power boost to fight the bosses in this tier compared to the previous one. So because of this, I would not recommend attempting to try this set of bosses until you are at least Level 275+ with Item Burning & Rental Gear.

Tier 7: Finale

Hard Black Mage, Hard Seren, & Hard Kai. With the 3 of these bosses having very similar HP, they act as the final bosses of Challenger World. Clearing all of these bosses at Level 280+ will grant you the Challenger rank.

Hard Black Mage could have technically been done between Easy Kalos & Easy Adversary, but that is only if you are willing to endure a 1 hour fight. I assume most people are not, so I put him in this tier with the expectation that you can solo him within about 30-35 minutes.

ALL of these bosses can be done with the existence of Item Burning & Rentals. However, there are some equipment slots that these 2 do not cover:

  • 3 Ring Slots
  • Pocket Item
  • 2nd Pendant
  • Secondary
  • Emblem
  • Medal

The New Item Burning

Item Burning has returned, and this time it can go up to 22 stars!!! However, there is a catch.

After you clear the Easy Lucid Mission, you will have 18 star gear. Previously it used to be 19 stars, but this time they lowered the amount of stars but increased the potentials on your armor by +3%.

Item Burning Stages 5 through 8 will not only require you to solo the boss that is listed, but you will also have to obtain Challenge Crest.

Challenge Crest is a currency used to bring your item burning to the next stage. Here is how much you need to upgrade the item burning gear:

19 Stars + Legendary Weapon: 600 Challenge Crest

20 Stars: 800 Challenge Crest

21 Stars: 1,000 Challenge Crest

22 Stars: 1,500 Challenge Crest

You need a total of 3,900 Challenge Crest to complete this Item Burning. I also want to point out that while the weapon gets a Legendary Potential of 40% Boss Damage, 30% IED, & 10% Attack, the weapon does not go beyond 18 stars.

Challenge Crest is obtained every week by defeating any weekly boss that is at the Hard Lucid stage or higher. Depending on which boss you clear, you will obtain a certain amount. It is extremely important to note that you will only receive the Challenge Crest one time, and the amount you get that week will depend on the hardest boss that you clear for that week.

For example, clearing out that entire bottom row does NOT give you 400 Challenge Crest, it only gives you 100. If you clear out that row and then run either Darknell or Verus Hilla, that 100 is replaced with 200. With the Item Burning event lasting 21 weeks, it will take you the entire duration of the event (20 weeks) to finish the gear if you do not progress past Hard Darknell or Verus Hilla.

The gear can be done in 2 months once you make it to Easy Adversary, and Hard Seren is heavily encouraged to clear because it will cut that time down in half, requiring only up to 4 weeks to clear the whole event.

Mystic Seal Tips

Everyday at reset, you get 3 Seal Control Rods. You don’t even have to be logged in to get them. However, you are not allowed to hold more than 20 Seal Control Rods. You will not earn any Seal Control Rods for the day if you already have 20.

The main goal of the Mystic Seal is to hit the Legendary Seal only once. Any other Seal you open you can dismantle for upgrade materials. Depending on your Challenger’s World Ranking, you will have a higher chance of hitting the Legendary Seal.

Stage 1: Less than Gold (0 points)

Stage 2: Gold (15,000 points)

Stage 3: Emerald (30,000 points)

Stage 4: Diamond (40,000 points)

Stage 5: Master (65,000 points)

Ideal Ranks/Tiers to “settle” on depending on Challenger Rank:

Stage 1: Rare (get to +6)

Stage 2: Epic (get to +6)

Stage 3: Unique (get to +6)

Stage 4: Unique or Legendary if you aren’t trying to sweat 65k yet

Stage 5: Legendary

The event shop sells each of these for 5m with a cap of 30 per week (Resets on Thursdays). You can hold onto them for 7 days before you have to use them.

The strategy is to hold out on using Rods until you absolutely have to (if the meso shop ones are about to expire or you are already at 20 Rods) to ensure the highest possible chance to reach Legendary as you progress your Challenger Rank. Once you reach Master, you can just use them all whenever you get them.

While waiting on your Legendary Seal, it’s perfectly fine to upgrade a lower tier seal up to +6 (MAYBE +7 as well but you don’t really have to). Afterwards you can save your remaining upgrade materials for when you hit the Legendary one.

I should also note that while the upgrading process is RNG, the stats on all Seals are the exact same (based on Tier and Enhancement Stage), for example, this means all Unique +7 seals that anyone can have will give the exact same stats (Main Stat, Secondary Stat, Attack, & Main Stat%)

Challenger EXP Duo

Gain EXP on your Challenger World and your Main World characters at the same time! You can only participate in this event by purchasing a ticket for 10,000 NX on your main world (cannot be purchased with maple points). The ticket is used to designate your current main world character as the recipient of this EXP.

Then on Challenger’s World, you will designate the character you are playing as the character that acquires the Duo Points by hunting monsters within your level range.

You can only purchase 1 EXP Duo Ticket every week. It will automatically be refreshed at a specific time every week (KMS was on weekly boss reset)

The Purchased ticket duration is 7 days, starting from the time you purchased it, you can hunt monsters within your level range to acquire the duo points.

You can use your duo points for 14 days starting from the time you used the ticket.

Unfortunately, the EXP Ticket and the Duo Point usage period cannot go past whenever Challengers World ends.

When you defeat monsters within your level on your Challengers World character, you will acquire Duo Points that can only be used on your main world. The amount of duo points acquired is based on the number of monsters you have defeated. You can acquire up to 1,500,000 Duo Points by defeating 645,000 monsters. Here is how the Duo Point acquisition works:

First 200,000 monsters: 4 Duo Points per mob

200,001 ~ 280,000: 3 Duo Points per mob

280,001 ~ 375,000: 2 Duo Points per mob

375,001 ~ 645,000: 1 Duo Point per mob

So despite needing to cap out the Duo Pass at 645k mobs, you earn more than half the points already at 200k mobs.

This is a rough list of what to expect from the Duo Pass. A level 291 character can earn 17.4 trillion EXP every week if they grind out 645,000 mobs on Challengers World. While this sounds nice, it is important to note that the expected hunting time to complete this every week is 31 hours, which any regular person is not capable of doing.

However, I would still find the Pass to be worth purchasing if you can reach the 200k mob mark, which can be done in almost 10 hours.

Basically, if you were interested in buying passes but you do not see yourself training for at least 10 hours in the next 7 days, you do not want to purchase this pass.

The Gearing Process

I think top priority for players who tend to actively play and grind should be Meso and Drop Gear by far. Because we have a very high amount of Normal Monster Damage this CW, you should be fine to use any accessories for this. Off the top of my head, this should include:

-Silver Blossom Ring

-Treasure Hunter John Ring (5mins of NLC questing)

-2 Event Rings (Can use more event rings to swap out Silver Blossom or Treasure Hunter John)

-Mechinator Pendant

-No second pendant ideally because of Spirit Pendant. Otherwise you can get Chaos Horntail Necklace.

Next is your WSE. Personally, I would leave the weapon slot alone until you liberate. Arcane Weapon is not a requirement for any bosses pre-lib. For your Secondary, you can just use the Frozen One provided early on. Personally, I am NOT waiting 2-3 weeks of PNo for a secondary that is only better by +6 Stat and +4 Attack to cube it. Secondary Lines are too good to justify losing so much damage early on to save for PNo. Try to aim for a 2L combination of Boss% + Attack% for the early game. You’ll probably be able to make a PNo secondary by the time you should be going for any 3L combination. With the implementation of the Astra Secondary, it really now officially no longer matters which secondary you choose if you plan on upgrading to that.

For your Emblem, as I mentioned, 1 IED line is fine at a low ranking but you’ll eventually want to swap that out. You can go for a 2L combination of Attack% + IED (BUT NO 2L IED), and go only for 2L Attack% or better once you rank up to at least Platinum.

For your Armor, the Item Burning will be more than enough early on and it will last you a very long time. As you start running Hard Arcane River bosses, you can slowly build your Arcane Set to phase out your item burning gear (Swap to Arcanes when all 4 armor pieces are 20~21 Stars + 2L Legendary).

There is a specific order in which you’ll want to use event titles. Here is how I feel like you should do it:

  1. Eternal Flame: The title you open as soon as you obtain your 1st Arcane Symbol after 5th job. You can immediately replace this title upon reaching Level 270 (DO NOT IMMEDIATELY CLAIM INFINITE FLAME AT LEVEL 240).
  2. Beyond Burning: The title you obtain after reaching Level 270 and finish the Beyond Burning Event. The stats are the exact same as Infinite Flame, but it gives 100 SAC instead of 50. This is far more important early on for progressing through early Grandis maps and bosses.
  3. Infinite Flame: Claim this only AFTER your Beyond Burning title expires (which should be 2 months later). From playing the character, you should have enough SAC by then to where the 50 SAC loss does not matter, and you will be able to have good damage stats for 4 months straight. Keep in mind even if the 2 months of Beyond Burning aren’t over for you yet, you should still claim and use this title right before the end of the Hyper Burning event period.

Item Burning Ignitia Set:

After you complete an Item Burning Mission, you will receive 2 Ignitia Keys. When you complete the first 4 missions, you will have all of the Ignitia Keys you need to transfer over to the Ignitia Set whenever you’d like.

It will apply while wearing any other equipment that is NOT Challenger’s, Eternals, or Sengoku. They act as a replacement to the exact set effect that you lost when you swapped out that piece of Challenger gear. This set effect will last until November 10, 2026.

In this screenshot, I am wearing 6 Item burning Equips and 2 Arcane Equips. I used the keys on the weapon slot and the glove slot to get 2 set Ignitia while still having 6 set Challenger. The 2 set Ignitia is equal to the stats of Set 7 and 8 on Challenger, so I was able to swap out to better gear without losing any set effect stats.

Farm 12 Star Speck Boxes a day, or as many as you can. Some people will be farming 12 a day on both their main world and Challenger’s World, but that’s a pretty insane fomo tactic. I would only do that if you actually feel like doing it and if not then it’s whatever

Pitched Whisper Crystals:

Farm and save as many Whisper Crystals as possible. People usually have a different preference on what they want to use it on. Personally, even though I was told not to, I do want to save my Crystals for a Genesis Badge if I ever feel like I won’t get it. After that, I would get whatever remaining pitches I did not receive as a drop.

Because there is now a way to reach 22 stars without booming (albeit very expensive) it is very feasible to reach at least 9 set in a realistic amount of time. The 10th item would either be refreshing Black Hearts or eventually a Total Control if I ever get one to drop.
